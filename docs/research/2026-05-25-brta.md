# Berkshire Regional Transit Authority (BRTA) — Verification Report

**Date:** 2026-05-25  
**Status at entry:** Tier 1A (desk screen, 2026-04-01)  
**Verdict:** **Confirmed Tier 1A — with one complexity flag**

---

## 1. Competitor Coverage

### Transit App
**Covered.** Transit App has a dedicated BRTA page at [transitapp.com/en/region/pittsfield/brta](https://transitapp.com/en/region/pittsfield/brta) listing all 13 routes with schedule data and "next departures." However, this is GTFS-schedule based, not a branded BRTA app. Transit App is a generic aggregator, not an operator-owned or operator-endorsed product.

### Moovit
**Covered.** [moovitapp.com](https://moovitapp.com/index/en/public_transit-lines-Berkshire_County_MA-5199-1082134) provides schedules and claims real-time data for BRTA routes (Routes 1, 2, 4, 5A, 11, 12, 14, etc.). Again, generic aggregator — not operator-branded.

### CityTransit
**Covered.** A third generic aggregator, [thecitytransit.com](https://thecitytransit.com/agency/berkshire%20regional%20transit%20authority/VcQ9uKVpNUpAvUPxNgRRXOD9lUSCwotUl5DEuxeE3VoZ-RfqK4DXMMT2W8JD3WMK), claims real-time BRTA data including service alerts. No operator relationship.

### Google Maps Transit
**Likely covered** via GTFS ingestion (BRTA's feed is on Transitland, fetched as recently as 2026-05-25), but no Google Maps-specific endorsement or confirmation found.

### Branded / Operator-Endorsed App
**No current branded app exists.** BRTA officially endorses two tools:
1. **RouteShout 2.0** — the legacy app from RouteMatch Software (iOS: 1.6★/248 ratings, last updated April 2019; App Store listing confirmed 2024-12-17 still showing active complaints).
2. **"Where's My B-Bus"** — a web tracker at `brta.routematch.io`, now under TripSpark/Modaxo ownership, which is currently active with service notices dated September 2025.

### MassDOT Unified App
**No unified RTA app exists at state level.** MassDOT coordinates 15 RTAs through the RTA Council but does not provide a single statewide transit app. The new Link413 intercity service (launched January 2026, connecting BRTA with FRTA and PVTA) uses MassDOT's BusPlus+ for ticketing, but this covers only that one intercity route, not BRTA local service.

### Recent Technology Contracts (last 18 months)
No app-specific RFP or technology vendor contract found. The December 2025 RFP ([RFP-2027-01](https://berkshirerta.gov/2025/12/30/rfp-2027-01-transit-management-services/)) covers transit management operations through 2029 but contains **zero mention of mobile app, ITS upgrade, or digital rider experience** — it is a pure operations management contract. New administrator Kathleen Lambert (started December 2025) has publicly discussed pursuing an ITS grant that would include a mobile ticketing and microtransit app, but as of this writing no contract has been awarded.

## 2. Documented Pain

### RouteShout App Rating (verified 2026-05-25)
- **iOS App Store:** 1.6★ / 248 ratings (up from 246 at original screen — still negligible growth, app effectively dead). Last update: April 8, 2019. Developer: RouteMatch Software, Inc. (now under TripSpark/Modaxo).

> "The app doesn't load in and when it does, it's kind of a rare occasion" — iOS reviewer, December 2024

> "Works about 5% of the time" — iOS reviewer, November 2019 (still the top-surfaced negative review, suggesting no meaningful fixes since)

> "Really cumbersome to use…takes many cumbersome steps to get to the information I need" — iOS reviewer, October 2017

### Service Quality Crisis (2025–2026)
BRTA is in an active operational crisis driven by a driver shortage — documented across Berkshire Eagle and the [traintime.substack.com](https://traintime.substack.com/p/how-the-brta-pittsfields-transit-system-lost-its-way) deep-dive (April 2026):

> "We're having a really hard time. Riders are being stranded when buses don't come." — Administrator Kathleen Lambert, February 2026 (via Traintime/Substack)

BRTA ran 26 drivers against a 36-driver requirement for full weekday service. Route cuts were proposed in early 2026, triggered public outcry, and were abandoned March 2026 — all within 4 months of new leadership arriving.

### Ridership (FY2024, confirmed via iBerkshires/NTD)
FY2024 fixed-route ridership exceeded FY2023 by **10.95%** and surpassed pre-COVID levels by **8.4%**. The Berkshire Eagle reported October 2024 ridership of **66,807 — a 30% year-over-year jump** for that month, partly driven by the fare-free program. Annual total unverified at exact number but consistent with the 800k/year figure; NTD profile page returned 403.

No app or technology coverage for this growth spike. Riders experiencing service reliability problems have no reliable digital tool to check real-time arrivals.

### Structural Pain: No Reddit Signal
Searches for Reddit discussions (r/berkshires, r/pittsfield) returned zero results for BRTA transit complaints — consistent with the demographic profile of a rural/small-city RTA where riders don't congregate on social media.

## 3. Operational Signals

### GTFS
- **Static feed URL:** `https://data.trilliumtransit.com/gtfs/berkshire-ma-us/berkshire-ma-us.zip` (via Transitland, [f-dre-berkshire~ma~us](https://www.transit.land/feeds/f-dre-berkshire~ma~us))
- **Feed freshness:** Fetched by Transitland on 2026-05-25 (~10 hours before verification). 49 historical versions in Transitland — feed has been maintained continuously.
- **GTFS-RT:** No dedicated GTFS-RT endpoint confirmed. BRTA uses RouteMatch/TripSpark backend (`brta.routematch.io`) which may expose RT internally, but no public GTFS-RT URL found. Moovit and CityTransit claim "real-time" data but may be scraping the RouteMatch web tracker, not consuming a standards-compliant GTFS-RT feed. **This is a gap to investigate during sales.**

### Routes and Coverage
13 fixed routes confirmed (all sources agree), 30 communities across Berkshire County. Advisory Board of 30-community representatives governs. MassDOT provides majority of operating funding.

### Decision Process
MassDOT-funded RTA → Advisory Board approval required for major contracts. New administrator Lambert (background: ARPA grant management, 30+ years transportation) is tech-positive and has specifically cited ITS/mobile as a strategic priority. She is actively pursuing grant funding — there is no locked vendor relationship.

### Ownership: TripSpark/RouteShout Chain
RouteMatch → acquired by Uber (2020) → acquired by Modaxo/TripSpark (2022). RouteShout app now technically "supported" under TripSpark but the iOS app has not been updated since 2019. The web tracker (`brta.routematch.io`) is maintained and shows September 2025 service notices. No migration off this stack has been announced.

---

## 4. Verdict

**Confirmed Tier 1A.**

BRTA passes on every criterion: live GTFS (refreshed today), 13 routes, ridership surging past 800k/year on fare-free tailwind, a branded app that is functionally abandoned (1.6★, last updated 2019, confirmed broken by December 2024 review), no dedicated third-party competitor (Moovit/Transit App are aggregators with no operator relationship), and no state-level unified solution.

**One complexity flag:** BRTA is in an organizational crisis — new administrator, failed route realignment, driver shortage, an incoming operations contractor (Keolis, July 2026). The instability cuts both ways: it makes technology a secondary priority right now, but the new administrator has explicitly named mobile/ITS as a strategic goal and is grant-hunting. The ITS grant pursuit is the opening.

**Primary wedge angle:** Administrator Lambert's stated ITS grant objective. She wants mobile ticketing + microtransit coordination + paratransit same-day booking. TransitKit can position as the rider-facing layer on top of their existing RouteMatch/TripSpark backend — a no-rip-and-replace story that fits the grant framing ("modernize without replacing your operations stack"). Secondary angle: the fare-free ridership spike with zero reliable app = a documented, quantifiable pain moment.

**Trigger to reassess downward:** If Keolis (the incoming operations contractor from July 2026) brings a bundled app as part of their management package — that would likely kill the opportunity. Monitor the RFP-2027-01 award announcement.
