# Citilink (Fort Wayne, IN) — Verification Report

**Date:** 2026-05-25
**Analyst:** Claude Code (deep verification pass)
**Prior tier:** 1A (desk screen, 2026-04-01)
**Verdict:** Downgraded to **Tier 1B**

---

## 1. Competitor Coverage

### Transit App
Fully integrated. Transit App has a dedicated regional page at [transitapp.com/en/region/fort-wayne/citilink](https://transitapp.com/en/region/fort-wayne/citilink) covering all 14 Citilink routes with real-time tracking. Crucially, Citilink **officially endorses the Transit App** as its real-time tracking vehicle: the November 2025 NAVINEO launch explicitly directed riders to download the Transit App for live bus locations.

> "Fort Wayne bus riders can now track their buses in real time thanks to a new partnership between Citilink and Systrans... available through the Transit app, allowing users to see exactly where their bus is, how long until it arrives and which routes to take to reach their destination." — [WANE 15, Nov 2025](https://www.wane.com/top-stories/citilink-launches-real-time-bus-tracking-system-for-fort-wayne-riders/)

The Transit App is not just a passive GTFS consumer here — it is the **official rider-facing tracking layer** per Citilink's own marketing.

### Moovit
Covers Fort Wayne fully. [moovitapp.com/index/en/public_transit-lines-Fort_Wayne_IN-3472-1590056](https://moovitapp.com/index/en/public_transit-lines-Fort_Wayne_IN-3472-1590056) lists all 21 route permutations with real-time data, stop maps, and service alerts. Not endorsed by Citilink, but fully functional.

### Google Maps Transit
Active. Citilink's trip planner at [fwcitilink.com/trip-planner/](https://fwcitilink.com/trip-planner/) is a Google Maps Transit embed. GTFS is ingested by Google and Apple Maps. Trip planning is available to any rider with a smartphone today, without a separate app.

### Branded Apps — What Exists

| App | Platform | Developer | Rating | Function | Last Updated |
|-----|----------|-----------|--------|----------|-------------|
| **Citilink Access** | iOS + Android | Via Transportation | 4.8 (30 ratings) | Demand-response paratransit booking only (ADA) | May 2026 |
| **Token Transit** | iOS + Android | Token Transit | N/A | Fare purchase + QR scan only — zero trip planning | Active |

There is **no branded Citilink fixed-route trip-planning or tracking app** for general riders. The "Citilink Access" app is paratransit-only (ADA). Confirmed.

### Real-Time Tracker Vendor
**Equans / Systrans NAVINEO CAD/AVL** — [announced December 2024](https://www.masstransitmag.com/technology/press-release/55248031/fort-wayne-citilink-citilink-introduces-token-transit-mobile-validators-on-buses-partners-with-equans-to-implement-its-navineo-cad-avl-solution), deployed summer 2025. The public-facing tracker URL is `swiv.citilink.cadavl.com/SWIV/CITILINK`. Previously used **DoubleMap**, [discontinued January 2, 2025](https://fwcitilink.com/doublemap-service-discontinued/). Before that, **TransLoc** (2021). Vendor churn is real.

**Key finding:** NAVINEO's rider output is routed through the Transit App, not a branded Citilink product. No GTFS-RT feed URLs are publicly documented on fwcitilink.com/gtfs/ — only the static GTFS zip is listed.

## 2. Documented Pain

### App Fragmentation (verified)
The rider journey as of May 2026 requires at minimum three separate tools:
1. **Google Maps** or **Transit App** — trip planning + live tracking
2. **Token Transit** — fare purchase
3. **fwcitilink.com/trip-planner/** — official but Google Maps-embedded; not mobile-native

Rider confusion is documented. From the Citilink Access Google Play listing, one review (Jan 2025):

> "EVERYONE WHO IS TRYING TO USE THIS APP FOR TRAVELING, DOWNLOAD THE TOKEN TRANSIT APP. THAT APP HAS THE VIRTUAL TICKETS, NOT THIS ONE. I CALLED THE NUMBER PROVIDED HERE AND THEY DIRECTED ME TO TOKEN TRANSIT."

Another review noted that registration required a phone call to complete — for a transit app in 2025.

### Funding Crisis (real, material)
From [Input Fort Wayne, June 2025](https://inputfortwayne.com/solving-a-shortfall-citlink-seeks-long-term-solutions-for-public-transportation-funding/): 2025 budget need $20.8M, shortfall ~$2.5M. Rainy day fund at $625K. First fare increase in over a decade (April 2025). Operating expenses +54% since 2013; state funding grew only +9%. Governor's proposed budget: 0% increase for 2026-2027.

This budget stress makes a paid technology contract harder to justify — and also creates urgency for efficiency tools.

### Reddit / Community Signals
No Reddit threads surfaced with direct rider app complaints (r/FortWayne searches returned no results). Limited community voice data. The 2025 Community Survey cited desires for Sunday service, later evening hours, and new employer connections — not app quality specifically.

## 5. Verdict

**Downgraded to Tier 1B.**

Citilink remains a genuine fragmentation story — three tools to ride a bus is real pain — but the April screen underweighted two disqualifying complications:

1. **Transit App is the official tracking partner.** Citilink is not looking for a tracking solution; they just signed one. Proposing a white-label app that competes with a vendor they publicly endorsed 6 months ago is a hard conversation, not an open door.

2. **GTFS-RT is not public.** NAVINEO's real-time data feeds through Transit App's proprietary pipeline. No open GTFS-RT endpoint means our RT proxy cannot ingest vehicle positions without a separate data agreement with Equans/Systrans — a deal we cannot assume.

3. **Scale is mid-tier, not small-operator.** At 2.05M riders/year, 51 buses, and an active multi-vendor tech stack, Citilink has enough organizational complexity that a lightweight white-label app is a harder sell than at a 5-route rural operator.

**Wedge angle if re-engaged:** Not consolidation of existing tools (Transit App just closed that door) — instead, position around the **paratransit gap**: Token Transit + Via (Citilink Access) are handling ADA paratransit, and the outsourcing RFP signals instability there. A unified rider account (fixed route + paratransit booking) is a real gap no current vendor closes.

**Trigger to restore to 1A:** NAVINEO/Transit App partnership dissolves, or Citilink issues an RFP specifically for a branded fixed-route mobile application. Monitor the "Transit Tomorrow" long-range plan outcome (expected 2026).
